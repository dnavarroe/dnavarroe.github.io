Customer Segmentation using KMeans Clustering

Description

A fashion retail chain is looking to enhance its marketing strategy and customer service. They want to personalize their offers and promotions for different customer segments with the aim of increasing sales and customer loyalty. To achieve this, they decide to use information about their customers' Annual Income and Spending Score to segment them more effectively.

Problem Description

The company faces the challenge of how to efficiently segment its customers using the available information about their Annual Income and Spending Score. Annual Income provides an insight into customers' purchasing power, while Spending Score indicates how much they are actually spending in the store. Properly segmenting customers into meaningful groups will enable the company to tailor its marketing and service strategies to meet the specific needs and preferences of each segment.

Available Data

  • Annual Income:The annual income of each customer, in k dollars.

  • Spending Score:A score assigned to each customer based on their spending habits, on a scale from 1 to 100, where 100 indicates the highest spending and 1 the lowest.

Objective

The main objective is to identify customer segments that share similar characteristics in terms of income and spending behavior. These segments may include customers with high incomes and high spending scores, customers with high incomes but low spending scores, customers with low incomes and high spending scores, etc.

Expected Benefits

  • Improvement in offer and promotion personalization.

  • Increase in sales by effectively targeting high-value segments.

  • Improvement in customer satisfaction and loyalty by addressing their specific needs.

  • Optimization of marketing and service resources by targeting more relevant segments.
